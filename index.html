LBRP: Firearms Handout

Firearms realted Sections,

Section 1,

Requirement of a firearm certificate it is an offence for a person: To have in their possession or to purchase or acquire, A firearm to which this section applies without holding a firearm certificate in force at the time, Or otherwise than as authorised by such a certificate.

Section 1A,

To have in their possession or to purchase or acquire, Any ammunition to which this section applies without holding a firearm certificate in force at the time, Or otherwise than as authorised by such a certificate, Or in quantities in excess of those so authorised.

Section 2,

Requirement of certificate for possession of shot guns. E+W+S. Subject to any exemption under this Act, it is an offence for a person to have in his possession, or to purchase or acquire, a shot gun without holding a certificate under this Act authorising him to possess shot guns.

Section 4,

Conversion of weapons: Subject to this section, It is an offence to shorten the barrel of a shotgun to a length less than 24 inches. A person has in there possession or under there control and article that is capable of being used ( Whether by itself or with other articles ) To convert an imitation firearm into a firearm.

Section 5,

Weapons subject to general prohibition: Any self-loading or pump-action rifled gun other than one which is chambered for .22 rim-fire cartridges Any rocket launcher, Or any mortar, For projecting a stabilised missile other than a launcher or mortar designed for line-throwing, pyrotechnic purposes or as a signalling apparatus.

Terrorism act 2000,

Section 41 - A constable may arrest without a warrant a person whom he reasonably suspects to be a terrorist.

Section 16,

Possession of a firearm or ammunition with intent to endanger life.

Section 16 A,

Possession of a firearm or imitation with intent to cause fear of violence.

Section 17(1),

Using a firearm or imitation to resist or prevent lawful arrest of himself or another.

Section 17(2),

Possessing a firearm or imitation whilst committing certain offences.

Section 18,

Carrying a firearm or imitation with intent to commit an indictable offence or to resist arrest or prevent the arrest of another.

Section 19,

Carrying a loaded shotgun, Air weapon, ( Whether loaded or not ) Any other firearm ( Whether loaded or not ) together with ammunition suitable for use in that firearm or an imitation firearm in a public place without lawful authority or reasonable excuse.

Section 20,

Entering a building or part of a building as a trespasser without reasonable excuse whilst having a firearm or imitation.

Section 21,

Possession of a firearm by persons previously convicted of crime.

Section 22(1),

Purchase or hire of any firearm or ammunition by a person under 18 years of age.

Section 24(1),

Selling or hiring an air weapon to a young person under the age of 18.

Section 24ZA,

A person in possession of an air weapon failing to take reasonable precautions to prevent a person under the age of 18 having the weapon with them.

Section 46,

Power to search with a warrant. To enter any premise or place named in the warrant, if necessary by force to search premise and place and anyone found on premise.

Section 47,

Powers of a constable to stop and search. A constable may require any person whom cause to suspect of having a firearm with or without ammunition with them being in a public place.

Section 49,

Police powers in relation to arms traffic. A constable may search and seize any firearms or ammunition which he has reason to believe are being removed or to have been removed in contravention.
